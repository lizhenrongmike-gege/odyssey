# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Founders and marketing leads at restaurants, hospitality brands, and growing consumer/B2B businesses (Bay Area and beyond) evaluating a marketing partner. They arrive via referral, social, or search — often on mobile — and want to quickly judge whether Cilo is credible, current, and capable of getting their brand in front of the right audiences. Job to be done: decide it's worth booking a call.

## Product Purpose
The marketing site for Cilo Media Studio LLC, a Bay Area social-first marketing agency (influencer & social campaigns, cold outbound, SEO, conversion optimization). It exists to communicate credibility and capability, convert visitors into "Book a call" leads, and satisfy two Meta gates (Business Verification + Instagram API app review) via consistent business identity and crawlable legal pages. Success = booked calls and a passed Meta review.

## Brand Personality
Modern, energetic, credible. Voice: warm and concrete, never hypey; confident without overclaiming (no fabricated metrics). It should feel like a sharp, social-native studio that also earns the trust of B2B buyers. Emotional goal: "these people are current, and they get results."

## Anti-references
- Generic AI-template SaaS: purple/indigo gradients, cream-and-slate restraint, identical icon-card grids, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section.
- Stuffy, monochrome, traditional corporate (the site's previous default-theme state).
- Editorial-magazine cosplay (display-serif + italic drop caps + ruled broadsheet columns) — Cilo is an agency, not a magazine.

## Design Principles
- Practice what we preach: a social-first agency's own site must look art-directed and current, not templated.
- Show the work is human: real restaurant/creative imagery over abstract shapes or empty color blocks.
- Confident, not loud: one committed accent and one display face carry the energy; everything else stays clean and legible.
- Earn B2B trust while feeling social-native: credible structure, energetic surface.
- Truthful: no invented metrics or testimonials; business identity consistent everywhere (Meta cross-checks it).

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1 (the cobalt accent is chosen/tested for contrast on the off-white surface and with white button text). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` (handled globally in reset.css). All business identity is selectable real text, never inside an image.
